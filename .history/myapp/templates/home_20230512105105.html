Aim

To build an application that can classify Bhutanese currency notes (except old). Along with that we also aim to identify counterfeit Indian notes.

About Project

Even with all the advancements in technology, paper currency is still needed for the vast majority of financial transactions today. Cash deals are subject to a lot of human error, though. Due to the inherent flaws in cash transactions, automated ways of handling money are increasingly prevalent in financial organizations like banks, vending machines, slot machines, automated teller machines (ATMs), etc. Typically, such automation includes the essential function of currency identification.

In addition to the need for automation, currency recognition systems could also benefit the disabled, particularly the blind. In Bhutan, where the majority of adults live in rural areas and the rate of illiteracy is extremely high, currency recognition systems could assist the blind in distinguishing between different Bhutanese banknotes. Additionally, the system will help tourists and foreigners learn to recognize the local currency and its worth on their own.
